Mohammad Ali

Mohammad Ali, also known as "The Greatest," was one of the most celebrated athletes of the 20th century. He was not only an incredible boxer, but also a civil rights activist and humanitarian. Born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. on January 17, 1942, in Louisville, Kentucky, he began his boxing career at the age of 12.

Ali's career quickly took off, and he went on to win a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome. He turned professional later that year and soon became known for his flamboyant personality and his ability to predict the round in which he would knock out his opponent.

In 1964, at the age of 22, Ali won the heavyweight championship of the world by defeating Sonny Liston. After the fight, he announced that he had converted to Islam and changed his name to Mohammad Ali. He also became known for his political activism, speaking out against the Vietnam War and refusing to be drafted into the military. As a result, he was stripped of his boxing titles and banned from the sport for several years.

Ali returned to the ring in 1970 and went on to have several more iconic fights, including the "Thrilla in Manila" against Joe Frazier in 1975. He retired from boxing in 1981 with a record of 56 wins, 5 losses, and 37 knockouts.

Outside of the ring, Ali continued to be a prominent figure in the civil rights movement and worked tirelessly to promote peace and understanding between people of different races and religions. He was also a strong advocate for Parkinson's disease research, which he was diagnosed with in 1984.

Throughout his life, Ali was known for his wit, charm, and charisma, as well as his incredible athleticism and boxing skills. He passed away on June 3, 2016, at the age of 74, but his legacy lives on as an inspiration to people all over the world.

In conclusion, Mohammad Ali was a true icon of the 20th century, a gifted athlete, and a champion of human rights and social justice. His legacy will continue to inspire future generations to fight for what they believe in, and to always strive for greatness both in and out of the ring.
